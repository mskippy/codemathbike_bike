Race #2 – Seymour Enduro

Enduro-style race at Mt. Seymour featuring multiple timed downhill stages.

  • Date: Wednesday, April 15
  • Location: Mt. Seymour / Old Buck Trailhead
  • Lead School: Handsworth
  • Registration: Parkgate Park (north end)
  • Parking:
    • Parkgate Park
    • Old Buck Trailhead
    • Along Anne Macdonald Way

Race Day Schedule

Time Details
2:00 PM Registration / chip pickup opens
2:30 PM Marshal briefing
2:45 PM Rider briefing (all riders)
3:00 PM Riders released in waves
  • 1st Wave – Grades 10/11/12
  • 2nd Wave – Grades 8/9
3:30 PM Timing starts

Riders should arrive early, check in, and be ready with all required gear.

Course Information

Course Update: Due to wet conditions and after the pre-ride, we will NOT be racing on Pangor.

Map Note: Trailforks is currently not cooperating, so route maps are not posted. Riders should rely on the descriptions below and the rider briefing.

Grade 8/9

Stages

  • Stage 1: John Deer (from the bell)
  • Stage 2: Empress Bypass

Route

Old Buck → Bridal Path → Academy Climb → RT Connector → Penny Lane → Good Sir Martin.

After Stage 1, riders return across Bridal Path, descend Empress Bypass, and return to Parkgate.

Grade 10/11/12

Stages

  • Stage 1: Pingu
  • Stage 2: Severed D
  • Stage 3: John Deer

Route

Old Buck → Powerline to Stage 1

Baden Powell → Mushroom → Powerline to Stage 2

Stage 2 ends at GSM → climb back up to Stage 3 (John Deer)

After Stage 3, riders return via Bridal Path but will exit via the Perimeter Trail to avoid conflict with Grade 8/9 riders.

Race Notes

  • Riders are responsible for knowing their course
  • Stay on designated routes
  • Be respectful of all trail users
  • Ride in control and within your ability
  • Helmets required at ALL times – no headphones
  • All timing chips must be returned

Marshal Information

Due to the course change, marshal locations will be slightly adjusted on race day. Please be flexible and follow instructions at the marshal briefing.

Marshal Briefing: 2:30 PM

Marshals help direct riders, manage trail access, and support safe race flow.

Key Responsibilities

  • Direct riders at key intersections
  • Manage trail crossings and public interaction
  • Support safe rider flow through stages

Important

  • Add your phone number when signing up – it will be used for race day communication
  • Be prepared to adjust positions based on course changes
  • Follow instructions from race organizers
